Fundamentals of
computer engineering

Fundamentals of Computer Engineering (FCE) aims to give a professional and an academical view of what computer engineering is. It provides a glance of the bases of this engineering and the role of a computer engineer in today's society.
The course is divided in two parts: the theoretical foundations and the development of practical skills.
The theoretical part covers computer engineering in society (background and historical perspective, computerengineering as an academic disciplone or profession, the human factor...), information and data (representation and organization of information) and information processing, managemente and transmission (fundamentals, current paradigms and trends in computrer systems and applications).
The second part focuses on the development of practical skills, introducing students to web development through the realization of a project.
This subject also contributes to develop paralel other kinds of skills such as teamwork, ethical commitment and communication skills of technical information or comprehensive training of the engineer.
How is the subject distributed?

MODULE I: THEORETICAL FOUNDATIONS

BLOCK 1. Computer engineering in society BLOCK 2. Current paradigms and trends in systems and applications BLOCK 3. Information and Data
  • Historical background and perspectives.
  • Definitions and basic concepts.
  • Past, present, and future of ICT (Information and Communication Technology).
  • Computer Engineering as an academic discipline and profession.
  • Software development: more than just programming.
  • Structure of computer systems: hardware, software, Operative systems and networks.
  • New paradigms and trends: Cloud computing, Quantum computing, Artificial Intelligence, Blockchain, Smart cities, IoT (Internet of Things).
  • Information and Data: number systems, representation of numeric and alphanumeric information (codes).
  • Data Foundations: Big Data, Data models, Data protection, Laws and ethics.

MODULE II: PRACTICAL SKILLS - INTRODUCTION TO WEB DEVELOPMENT

BLOCK 1. Introduction and management of software repositories BLOCK 2. Creation of HTML5 web pages BLOCK 3. Design and layout with CSS 3
  • Basic concepts.
  • Repositories: Git & Github.
  • Basic concepts.
  • The Document Object Model.
  • Forms and containers.
  • Basic concepts.
  • CSS elements.
  • Box and float models.
  • Element positioning.

When successfully passing the subject, the student will be expected to be able:

  • To recognize and employ, in an explanation or discourse, the terminology, both hardware and software, involved in the design, management, and operation of a computer system, relating to any field of knowledge within computer engineering.
  • To narrate and establish connections between key historical facts and events in the emergence and evolution of computers and computer science, as well as to present and analyze future trends.
  • To explain the foundations and apply different mechanisms for the representation and organization of both numerical and non-numerical information within a computer system.
  • To explain the basic aspects of the fundamental areas of Computer Engineering: hardware, software, and networks.
  • To develop a web application for the purpose of disseminating information.
  • To conduct research, create, and effectively present a research project, utilizing both oral and written communication skills.
